2017 - present: I am a Ph.D. student within the HCI Lab at the University of Waterloo, advised by Dr. Edith Law. The focus of my Ph.D. is on designing interactions of pedagogical conversational agents, from chatbots to physically-embodied robots, that promote social experiences.

2014 - 2016: M.Sc. cum laude Human-Machine Communication, University of Groningen, the Netherlands (Department of Artificial Intelligence at the Bernoulli Institute).
My research focused on improving voice perception for cochlear implant wearers, developing an EEG brain-computer interface for neurofeedback, as well as conducting psychoacoustic experiments to investigate how the comparison and integration of the different sound signals in bimodal hearing impacts perception of voice characteristics in speech.

2014 - 2016: Honours Master High Tech Systems and Materials

2009 - 2014: B.Sc. Cognitive Systems: Cognition and Brain, University of British Columbia, Canada.

Awards
2019: Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC) Postgraduate Scholarship-Doctoral (PGS D)
2014: Avril McDonald Prize, University of Groningen
